Stir-fry

A quick and easy Asian-inspired dish typically made with vegetables, meat, or tofu, saut√©ed in a wok with a flavorful sauce.

To make a simple stir fry, start by preparing your ingredients: slice your choice of vegetables (such as bell peppers, broccoli, carrots, and snow peas) and protein (such as chicken, beef, shrimp, or tofu). Heat a wok or large skillet over high heat and add a small amount of oil with a high smoke point, like vegetable or sesame oil. Once the oil is hot, add your protein and stir-fry until it's nearly cooked through. Remove the protein from the wok and set it aside.

In the same wok, add a bit more oil if needed and toss in the vegetables. Stir-fry the vegetables until they are crisp-tender, then return the protein to the wok. Pour in a sauce made from soy sauce, oyster sauce, garlic, ginger, and a bit of sugar or honey. Stir everything together and cook until the sauce is well combined and heated through. Serve the stir fry over steamed rice or noodles, garnished with sesame seeds or chopped green onions.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp vegetable or sesame oil
  • 1 lb protein of choice (chicken, beef, shrimp, or tofu), sliced
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (bell peppers, broccoli, carrots, snow peas)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, minced
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp sugar or honey
  • Cooked rice or noodles for serving
  • Sesame seeds or chopped green onions for garnish

Steps

  1. Prepare your ingredients by slicing the vegetables and protein.
  2. Heat a wok or large skillet over high heat and add 1 tbsp of oil.
  3. Add the protein to the hot oil and stir-fry until nearly cooked through. Remove and set aside.
  4. In the same wok, add another tbsp of oil if needed and add the vegetables. Stir-fry until crisp-tender.
  5. Return the protein to the wok and add the garlic and ginger. Stir-fry for another minute.
  6. Pour in the soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sugar or honey. Stir to combine and cook until heated through.
  7. Serve the stir fry over steamed rice or noodles, garnished with sesame seeds or chopped green onions.
