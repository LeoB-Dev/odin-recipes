Roast Dinner

A traditional British meal consisting of roasted meat, potatoes, and accompaniments such as Yorkshire pudding, vegetables, and gravy.

To make a classic roast dinner, start by selecting your meat of choice, such as beef, chicken, lamb, or pork. Season the meat with salt, pepper, and herbs like rosemary or thyme, then place it in a roasting pan. Surround the meat with peeled and chopped potatoes, carrots, and parsnips, tossing them in a bit of oil and seasoning. Roast the meat and vegetables in a preheated oven at 180째C (350째F) until the meat reaches the desired internal temperature and the vegetables are tender and golden brown.

While the meat rests, make the gravy by using the drippings from the roasting pan. Place the pan on the stove, add a bit of flour to the drippings, and cook, stirring constantly, to form a roux. Gradually add stock, continuing to stir until the gravy thickens. Serve the sliced meat and roasted vegetables with the gravy, Yorkshire puddings, and steamed green vegetables like peas or broccoli for a complete roast dinner.

Ingredients

  • 1.5-2 kg meat of choice (beef, chicken, lamb, or pork)
  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 3 carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 3 parsnips, peeled and chopped
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh rosemary or thyme
  • 2 tbsp flour
  • 500ml beef or chicken stock
  • Yorkshire puddings
  • Steamed green vegetables (peas, broccoli)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 180째C (350째F).
  2. Season the meat with salt, pepper, and herbs, and place it in a roasting pan.
  3. Surround the meat with the chopped potatoes, carrots, and parsnips. Toss the vegetables in oil and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Roast in the preheated oven until the meat reaches the desired internal temperature and the vegetables are tender and golden brown.
  5. Remove the meat from the oven and let it rest while you prepare the gravy.
  6. Place the roasting pan on the stove over medium heat. Add the flour to the drippings and cook, stirring constantly, to form a roux.
  7. Gradually add the stock, stirring until the gravy thickens.
  8. Serve the sliced meat and roasted vegetables with the gravy, Yorkshire puddings, and steamed green vegetables.
